# Voice Profiles — Into the Abyss Podcast

Use these profiles when generating voices with TTS tools (ElevenLabs, NotebookLM, etc.).

---

## Host — "Alex"

**Role:** The interviewer. A card game enthusiast and gaming podcast veteran who's done their homework on Echo of Abyss and asks sharp, informed questions.

**Voice characteristics:**
- Male, late 20s to mid 30s
- Warm baritone, clear and well-projected
- American English, neutral accent (no strong regional flavor)
- Polished but not stiff — sounds like someone who's been on mic before
- Upbeat energy, genuinely curious, leans forward into questions

**Delivery style:**
- Medium pace, slightly faster than conversational — keeps momentum
- Rises in pitch slightly at the end of questions to signal genuine curiosity, not just reading a list
- Brief pauses before pivoting to a new topic, giving the conversation room to breathe
- Occasionally emphasizes a game term with a slight punch ("five slots," "eleven-point budget") to highlight design details
- Laughs naturally when something is funny, doesn't force it

**Emotional range:**
- Default: engaged, interested, warm
- When reacting to a design insight: impressed but not over-the-top ("oh, that's clever")
- When setting up a challenging question: slightly playful, like a friend poking at an idea
- When wrapping a segment: slightly slower, more grounded, bringing the energy down for a clean transition

**TTS prompt suggestion:**
> A warm, clear male voice in his early 30s. Sounds like a professional podcast host — confident, articulate, and genuinely curious. Medium pace, conversational but polished. American English, neutral accent. Friendly energy without being hyperactive. Think tech podcast host who actually plays the games he covers.

---

## Dev — "Thunder"

**Role:** The creator. A lifelong card game player who's building his dream game solo with AI tools. Passionate, thoughtful, occasionally self-deprecating.

**Voice characteristics:**
- Male, late 20s to mid 30s
- Mid-range voice, natural and grounded
- Slight accent is fine — should sound like someone for whom English is fluent but maybe not their first language (adds authenticity)
- Less "broadcast polished" than the Host — sounds like a real person talking about something they love

**Delivery style:**
- Slightly slower pace than the Host — more thoughtful, like someone choosing their words as they go
- Gets faster and more animated when excited about a design idea or telling a story
- Uses natural filler occasionally ("honestly," "right," "like") — not robotic or over-rehearsed
- Pauses briefly before answering big questions, as if actually thinking about it
- Drops into a lower, more casual register for asides and jokes ("at least I think so")

**Emotional range:**
- Default: calm, confident, reflective
- When explaining a mechanic: focused and clear, almost like a teacher who loves the subject
- When telling a story (Yu-Gi-Oh, the Cyclone moment): warmer, more animated, pace picks up
- When being honest about difficulty ("art is hard physically"): grounded, matter-of-fact, no self-pity
- When delivering the elevator pitch or something they're proud of: quiet confidence, not boastful

**TTS prompt suggestion:**
> A natural, grounded male voice in his early 30s. Sounds like a passionate indie game developer talking casually about his project — thoughtful, genuine, not overly polished. Medium-slow pace that speeds up when excited. Slight warmth and occasional dry humor. Should feel like a real person in a real conversation, not a voice actor reading lines.

---

## Interaction dynamic

The two voices should contrast enough to be instantly distinguishable:
- **Host** is the one driving forward — slightly higher energy, asks the questions, keeps the pace
- **Dev** is the one going deeper — slightly lower energy, gives the substance, takes his time

When both are engaged in a back-and-forth (especially in Segments 2 and 3), the pacing should feel like two friends at a coffee shop, not a formal interview. The Host's questions pull energy up; the Dev's answers settle it back down with substance. That rhythm is what makes the conversation feel natural.
